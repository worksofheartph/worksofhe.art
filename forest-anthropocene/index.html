The Anthropocene is a new geological age that focuses on the dynamics between human and nature that impact our climate and environment. In this very age—the Philippines has gradually and significantly lost its tropical forests, with the evidence of mankind’s actions like deforestation and forest degradation.


Forest Foundation aims, through this book, to provide local accounts of human and forest dynamics, and probe into the historical trajectories and conservation experiences within the forest landscapes in the Philippines.
TELLING THE STORIES OF OUR FORESTS

Forest Foundation Philippines is a non-profit organization that provides opportunities to other associations who share the same vision of protecting the forests. As part of the Foundation’s Learning Landscapes Program, they assembled a multidisciplinary research team to create Forests in the Anthropocene: Perspectives from the Philippines, a resource book for individual professionals and organizations that records local accounts on the human and forest dynamics. It spotlights conservation processes and experiences in a historical timeline to track and be able to pinpoint certain approaches that helped or hindered the state of our forests today.

How can we re-establish a better interaction with our environment? We need to go back a page, take a step back, and understand where we went wrong, where we did good, and where we can do better. Forests in the Anthropocene focuses on each interdisciplinary perspective in this epoch. Each chapter delves deeper into understanding social experiences, ushering sustainability issues, sharing perspectives on action that can be taken in governance, and various contributions of impact to the forests.

The book introduces us with the overview of the human and nature dynamics, with social, political, economic, and cultural influences taking part in the forests’ degradation. It leads us to understanding the challenges that are becoming more apparent and how we can put the human nature touch we have for the environment. Different analyses and case studies on community-based forestry initiatives, research assessments on ecological integrity, and more about the forest landscape are conducted around the Philippine regions. This book is to advocate further research on the anthropocene.

DEEP-ROOTED DESIGN, TRACING OUR GROWTH

It was important to balance the urgency and seriousness of the topic with a sense of hope—once we are equipped with the lessons of our past and the trends of our present, we have the capacity to change the future of our forests.

It's about the people in the environment; it's about the environment for the people.

To visually represent this message, we anchored the creative direction of the book on tree rings. The book starts with history and how it greatly affected the environment in the Philippines over time. It talks about indigenous people’s knowledge and relationship with the forests. What they know about the environment from past respect and care can help us conserve the forests in the present. Besides showing us a picture of our history, it also give us an idea of our future.

We wanted the design to represent not just the environment, but people and its relationship with the forest as well. When the book is held, the reader's thumb aligns with the center of the tree rings on the cover, emphasizing humankind's imprint and influence on the natural world.

Images and visual cues taken from the trip to the Yangil Nursery informed our creative direction and inspired the formation of our logo and assets.

Textured lines inspired by the patterns in tree rings and a technical grid combine to show both the indigenous/intrinsic knowledge and scientific expertise that are equally necessary in conserving our forests.

Taking visual cues from the creative direction of the book, the website was designed with the same intricacies of tree rings. It’s also an important touchpoint to create as it supplements people’s experience in understanding the forests and the anthropocene through an immersive and seamless experience. It houses the downloadable version of the actual book.

EPILOGUE AND PROLOGUE

This project aims to offer an accessible lens to the future we can make, where anyone can support and implement the conservation of our tropical forests, as well as keep our natural resources, community livelihood, and socio-economical solutions abundant.

In this age of man, how can we protect our forests? How can humankind be attentive and affectionate to nature?

We encourage policymakers, researchers, and students to promote more breathable forests and abundance. The book is available as a resource to further research on conservation, and as a course that can be used to consider policy recommendations.

Forests in the Anthropocene, in its current form as a book or publication, is finite—it has a last page—but everyone who worked in this project has an extended role to spread the content within its pages and reach those people who we need, who our forests need the most.
